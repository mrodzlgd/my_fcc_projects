Marie Curie

Polish-born French physicist famous for her work on radioactivity. First woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win the award in two different fields (Physics and Chemistry).

Timeline of Marie Curie's life

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Congress Kingdom of Poland, Russian Empire.
  • 1883 - Won a medal as top student of her secondary education class. Started to work as a teacher to finance her sister’s medical studies in Paris.
  • 1891 - Moved to Paris to study at The Sorbonne to study physics, chemistry, and mathematics.
  • 1893 - She obtained her Licentiateship in Physical Sciences.
  • 1894 - She obtained her Licentiateship in Mathematical Sciences. Met Pierre Curie who was completing a Doctorate of Science.
  • 1895 - Marie married Pierre Curie.
  • 1896 - Henri Becquerel found a phenomenon where the element uranium gives off unusual rays of energy. After studying it, Marie named the phenomenon radioactivity.
  • 1897 - Marie and Pierre had their first daughter, Irene.
  • 1898 - The Curies announced their discovery of two new elements, radium and polonium.
  • 1900 - Marie was appointed lecturer in physics at the École Normale Supérieure for girls in Sèvres where she introduced a method of teaching based on experimental demonstrations.
  • 1903
    • Marie became the first woman in France to receive a doctoral degree.
    • Marie and Pierre shared with Henri Becquerel the Nobel Prize for Physics for the discovery and research of radioactivity.
    • Marie became the first woman to receive a Nobel Prize
  • 1904 - Marie and Pierre had their second daughter, Eve.
  • 1906 - Pierre died in a traffic accident, Marie took over his professorship, and became the first woman to teach at The Sorbonne.
  • 1910 - Marie’s fundamental treatise on radioactivity was published.
  • 1911 - Marie is awarded the Nobel Prize for Chemistry, for the isolation of pure radium.
  • 1914 - Marie oversaw the completion of the building of the laboratories of the Radium Institute at the University of Paris. When World War I began, Marie, with the help of her daughter Irene, devoted herself to making X-ray technology available for use in the medical treatment of wounded soldiers.
  • 1921 - Accompanied by her two daughters, Marie Curie made a journey to the United States, where President Warren G. Harding presented her with a gram of radium bought as the result of a collection among American women.
  • 1922 - Marie focused her research on the chemistry of radioactive substances and the medical applications of these substances.
  • 1932 - Marie helped open the Radium Institute in Warsaw.
  • 1934 - On July 4, Marie died near Sallanches, France. Her death was the result of leukemia caused by exposure to radiation.
  • 1944 - American chemists discovered a new element. They named it curium in honor of Marie and Pierre Curie.
